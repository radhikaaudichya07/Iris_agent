# Iris — Exact Recording Runbook (say this / show this)

Total ≈ 2:30. Read the **SAY** lines out loud (or use as captions). Do the **SCREEN** actions.
Speak slowly and clearly. It's fine that Iris takes ~2–4 seconds to reply — leave it in, it proves it's real.

Windows to have open BEFORE recording:
- Browser tab: https://iris-agent-8msr.onrender.com/  (landing) and a second tab on /architecture
- Slack: #product-metrics (with @iria invited), prefs reset, chart-v1.png & chart-v2.png on desktop

---

## SCENE 1 — HOOK  (0:00–0:22)  · SCREEN: the landing page (top/hero)
**SCREEN:** Start on the landing page hero. Scroll slowly down to the "before/after" cards, then stop.
**SAY:**
> "One in six people live with a disability — and the EU Accessibility Act is now law. But every chart
> and screenshot posted in Slack is invisible to blind teammates, because alt-text is optional and nobody
> adds it. This is Iris — a Slack agent that makes every image accessible, automatically."

---

## SCENE 2 — CONTEXT (the hero moment)  (0:22–0:52)  · SCREEN: switch to Slack
**SCREEN:** In #product-metrics, click into a message's thread. Type this and send:
> `Are we on track for the APAC target of $3.5M this quarter?`
Then drag **chart-v1.png** into that same thread and send it. Wait for Iris to reply.
**SAY (while it's thinking):**
> "Watch what happens when someone asks a question and drops a chart. Iris doesn't just caption the image —
> it reads the data, and answers the actual question being discussed."
**SCREEN:** When Iris replies, hover/highlight the first line of its reply.
**SAY:**
> "'Yes — APAC is 4.2 million, 20% over target.' That's not a caption. That's understanding."

---

## SCENE 3 — MEMORY / DIFF (the whoa)  (0:52–1:18)  · SCREEN: still Slack
**SCREEN:** Post **chart-v2.png** in the channel. Wait for Iris to reply.
**SAY (while thinking):**
> "Teams post the same dashboard every day. So the next day, Iris doesn't repeat itself — it remembers,
> and tells you only what changed."
**SCREEN:** Highlight Iris's "Change since last time" reply.
**SAY:**
> "'Change since last time — APAC dropped from 4.2 to 3.0 million, now below the target.' A diff, from
> memory — more useful than what a sighted person gets glancing at the chart."

---

## SCENE 4 — PERSONALIZATION (the product)  (1:18–1:42)  · SCREEN: Slack DM
**SCREEN:** Open a direct message with @iria. Type and send:
> `keep it short`
Wait for Iris's confirmation. Then go back to #product-metrics and post **chart-v1.png** again.
**SAY:**
> "Every person reads differently. So just tell Iris — 'keep it short.' It remembers. And now, for me,
> the same image comes back as one tight line. It learned how I read."

---

## SCENE 5 — IMPACT / COMPLIANCE  (1:42–2:05)  · SCREEN: the Iris App Home
**SCREEN:** Click the Iris app in the sidebar → open its **Home** tab. Point at the Impact line.
**SAY:**
> "And Iris proves its impact — images made accessible, and hours of manual alt-texting saved. For an
> organization, that's automated progress toward accessibility law — the EU Accessibility Act and the ADA —
> with a number you can actually show."

## SCENE 6 — ARCHITECTURE + CLOSE  (2:05–2:30)  · SCREEN: browser /architecture, then landing close
**SCREEN:** Switch to the browser tab on /architecture. Slowly pan across the diagram (~6 seconds).

**SAY:**
